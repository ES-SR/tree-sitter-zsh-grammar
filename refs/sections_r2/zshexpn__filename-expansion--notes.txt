     Filename expansion is performed on the right hand side of a parameter
     assignment, including those appearing after commands of the typeset
     family.  In this case, the right hand side will be treated as a
     colon-separated list in the manner of the PATH parameter, so that a ‘~' or
     an ‘=' following a ‘:' is eligible for expansion.  All such behaviour can
     be disabled by quoting the ‘~', the ‘=', or the whole expression (but not
     simply the colon); the EQUALS option is also respected.

     If the option MAGIC_EQUAL_SUBST is set, any unquoted shell argument in the
     form ‘identifier=expression' becomes eligible for file expansion as
     described in the previous paragraph.  Quoting the first ‘=' also inhibits
     this.
